# About

I’m a systems and reliability engineer with a background spanning three decades of infrastructure evolution, with the last 15+ years operating at what would now be considered a Senior/Staff level.

My work has consistently centered around reliability, recovery, and understanding how systems behave under stress — from early infrastructure environments through modern platform and distributed systems.

## How I Work

I tend to approach engineering from a systems perspective rather than a task perspective.

That means:

- identifying failure modes early and designing them out
- reducing operational noise instead of normalizing it
- treating observability and debugging as first-class system features
- favoring deterministic, explainable behavior over implicit or “magic” systems

## What That Looks Like in Practice

In practice, this has meant:

- operating and maintaining production environments at significant scale
- designing private-cloud infrastructure and internal platforms
- building tooling that improves reproducibility and reduces ambiguity
- driving down alert fatigue and operational overhead through better system design

## Working Philosophy

I don’t view reliability as something you add after the fact.

It’s a property of how the system is designed.

Most of my work is focused on shifting effort from reacting to failures toward preventing them from existing in the first place.

## Outside Work

Outside of work, I’ve spent a significant amount of time working with and socializing a Nile monitor lizard — an experience that reinforced patience, structured iteration, and respecting systems, biological or technical, on their own terms.
